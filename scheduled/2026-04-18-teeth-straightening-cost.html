Teeth straightening cost guide

How much does teeth straightening cost in 2026?

Updated April 18, 2026 8–10 min read Sources: ADA · AAO
Bottom line: Teeth straightening costs $1,500–$10,000 depending on severity and method. At-home aligners are the cheapest at $1,500–$2,500 but are only appropriate for very minor corrections. Braces and Invisalign run $3,000–$9,000 for moderate-to-complex cases. Insurance typically covers $1,000–$2,000 of any orthodontic treatment.

Are at-home aligners safe?

At-home aligners like Byte, Candid, and SmileDirectClub are only appropriate for very minor tooth movement — primarily cosmetic crowding of front teeth. They should not be used for bite correction, significant spacing, or any case involving jaw alignment. Without regular in-person X-rays and exams, problems like root resorption (roots shortening) can go undetected.

If you're considering at-home aligners, get a consultation with an in-person orthodontist first to confirm your case is appropriate for remote treatment.

How to choose the right straightening method

The right option depends on how complex your case is. A free consultation with an orthodontist (most offer them) will give you a professional assessment. Key questions to ask:

  • Is my case appropriate for clear aligners, or do I need braces?
  • What is the estimated treatment duration?
  • What does the total fee include (retainers, follow-up visits)?
  • What payment plans are available?

Frequently asked questions

What is the fastest way to straighten teeth? +
For mild cases, Invisalign Lite or at-home aligners can show results in 3–6 months. Accelerated orthodontics using vibration devices (like Propel or VPro) can speed up braces or Invisalign treatment by 20–50% when combined with traditional treatment.
Can I straighten my teeth as an adult? +
Absolutely. Adults represent a growing share of orthodontic patients — roughly 1 in 4 orthodontic patients are adults. The options are the same as for teens, though treatment may take slightly longer. Many adults prefer clear aligners for their discreet appearance.
Do I need retainers forever after straightening? +
Yes, indefinitely — though not all day. After the active phase, teeth will slowly shift back toward their original positions without retention. Most orthodontists recommend wearing retainers every night long-term. Retainer replacement costs $100–$300 when they wear out or are lost.
